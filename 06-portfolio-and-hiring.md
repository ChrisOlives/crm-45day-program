## WHAT MAKES A STRONG PORTFOLIO ARTIFACT

Not all portfolio artifacts are equal. Here's how to evaluate yours:

**Tier 1 — Most Impressive (use these in interviews and applications)**
- Loom videos showing live systems working
- Architecture diagrams (Canva) showing the full picture
- Written RevOps reports with real analysis and recommendations
- n8n AI workflow exports (.json files employers can actually import)
- Lead scoring model documentation with rationale

**Tier 2 — Strong Supporting Evidence**
- Dashboard screenshots with real data
- Workflow canvases showing multi-step logic
- Data dictionaries and SOPs
- Migration field mapping documents

**Tier 3 — Completeness Proof (shows thoroughness)**
- Import CSVs and test data
- Testing logs and health audit checklists
- Individual workflow screenshots
- Certification badges

## Readiness Scale

| Level | Definition |
|---|---|
| Beginner | Can navigate HubSpot but cannot configure it independently |
| Junior | Can set up basic properties, pipelines, and simple workflows with guidance |
| Intermediate | Can configure a full CRM, build multi-step workflows, and produce reports independently |
| Employable | Hireable for CRM Specialist, Marketing Automation Specialist, or Junior RevOps roles |
| Deployment-Ready | Can be handed a real client account on Day 1 and deliver results without handholding |

## Evaluation 1: HubSpot Hiring Manager

**Role:** HubSpot Administrator (Mid-Market SaaS Company)
**Salary Range:** $55,000 – $75,000

**Reviewing your portfolio...**

### Strengths

**CRM Architecture Depth**
"Most candidates who apply for HubSpot Admin roles have used HubSpot but never designed a system from scratch. This portfolio shows architectural thinking — starting with a data model, designing lifecycle stages before touching workflows, and documenting everything. That's rare at this level."

**Multi-Industry Coverage**
"I'm looking at work across a cleaning company, real estate agency, SaaS startup, and e-commerce brand. That tells me this person can adapt their HubSpot configuration to different business contexts — not just copy-paste what they learned in one industry."

**Documentation Quality**
"The data dictionaries, pipeline SOPs, and workflow references are better than what I've seen from candidates with 2–3 years of experience. A new hire who can produce this quality of documentation is immediately valuable because they're not creating technical debt."

**Lead Scoring Implementation**
"Building a two-dimensional scoring model (fit + engagement) for the SaaS company is advanced. Most junior candidates don't know the difference between a fit score and an engagement score. This person clearly understands RevOps thinking, not just HubSpot mechanics."

### Weaknesses

**Limited Live Production Proof**
"Everything here is simulated on demo/sandbox accounts. I'd want to see at least one example of working on a real company's data, even if it was a small freelance project. The next step after this program is to take a real client — even for free — to have one 'real' case study."

**Reporting Sophistication**
"The dashboards look solid, but I don't see custom calculated reports or attribution reports using real multi-touch data. With simulated data, attribution reports don't tell me much. This is a minor gap — it's filled quickly with real account access."

### Interview Questions I Would Ask
1. "Walk me through how you'd set up HubSpot from scratch for a new client. Start from account creation."
2. "One of our sales reps says the CRM data is a mess. What are the first 3 things you'd audit?"
3. "How would you explain lifecycle stages to a sales rep who doesn't want to use them?"
4. "A workflow broke and leads aren't being created. How do you diagnose it?"

**Hiring Decision: STRONG YES for junior-to-mid HubSpot Admin role. Would require 30-day ramp with production account access.**

**Role:** Revenue Operations Analyst
**Company:** B2B SaaS, Series B, 80 employees
**Salary Range:** $70,000 – $95,000

**Reviewing your portfolio...**

### Strengths

**Systems Thinking**
"The GrowthBridge capstone shows full-funnel thinking. This person didn't just build features — they built a system. The architecture document with objectives, tech stack, integration map, and governance rules is what a RevOps analyst should be thinking about, not just 'how do I build this workflow.'"

**Cross-Functional Knowledge**
"Most RevOps candidates are strong in one area — marketing OR sales OR service. This portfolio covers all three: lead nurture sequences, sales pipeline automation, SLA enforcement, service ticketing, and customer health scoring. That breadth is exactly what RevOps demands."

**Data Governance Deliverable**
"The Stackr data governance SOP and the migration field mapping document are legitimately impressive. Data quality is the #1 pain point in every CRM I've ever managed. Someone who thinks proactively about governance before problems occur is senior-level thinking."

**RevOps Report Quality**
"The Q1 RevOps Optimization Report with bottleneck identification and specific HubSpot fix recommendations shows analytical maturity. I can see this person presenting findings to a VP of Sales. That's the core output of this role."

### Weaknesses

**No CRM-to-CRM Migration Experience**
"The Pipedrive simulation is good, but I'd want to see experience with a Salesforce → HubSpot migration or a real large-scale data migration. For a Series B company with complex data, this gap matters."

**No Experience with HubSpot Custom Objects**
"Our company uses custom objects extensively (we have a 'Subscriptions' object and a 'Projects' object). This portfolio doesn't demonstrate custom object architecture. It's an Operations Hub Pro feature — worth learning next."

**No A/B Testing or Experimentation**
"RevOps at a SaaS company involves constantly testing assumptions — A/B testing email sequences, testing pipeline stage definitions, testing scoring models. I don't see evidence of a test-and-iterate mindset here yet."

### Interview Questions I Would Ask
1. "Describe your process for designing a lead scoring model from scratch. How would you validate it's working?"
2. "What's the difference between a contact-based workflow and a deal-based workflow? When would you use each?"
3. "Our MQL→SQL conversion rate is 18%. What could be causing that to be low and how would you diagnose it?"
4. "How would you build a system that prevents marketing and sales from arguing about lead quality?"

**Hiring Decision: YES for RevOps Analyst role. Would want to see 1 real client project added before applying to Senior RevOps roles. Strong candidate pipeline.**
