# 45-Day CRM & Automation Career Shift Program
## Learning Strategy & 45-Day Roadmap

---

## CURRICULUM SPLIT DECISION: HubSpot 32 Days + n8n 13 Days

### Why This Split?

**The market reality:** 90% of CRM and RevOps job postings list HubSpot as required or preferred. n8n is increasingly valued but almost always listed as a "bonus" or "nice to have" — except at tech-forward companies. By spending 32 days on HubSpot, you become genuinely hireable as a specialist. By adding 13 focused days on n8n, you differentiate yourself from the average candidate and qualify for higher-paying automation-heavy roles.

**The logic behind 32 days of HubSpot:**
- HubSpot has 5 core Hubs (Marketing, Sales, Service, CMS, Operations) — each requires real hands-on time
- Employers test HubSpot knowledge in interviews with specific scenario questions
- A HubSpot portfolio without workflows, pipelines, dashboards, AND documentation is incomplete
- HubSpot certifications (free) add resume credibility — you'll earn 6+ during this program

**The logic behind 13 days of n8n:**
- 13 days is enough to build 8–10 production-quality workflows
- n8n skills + HubSpot skills = "Marketing Automation Specialist" or "RevOps Engineer" title eligibility
- n8n's visual workflow builder transfers directly to Zapier, Make.com, and other no-code tools
- AI-powered n8n workflows are a competitive differentiator in 2025 hiring

## LEARNING PHILOSOPHY

### Theory-Minimum, Project-Maximum
Every concept is introduced only because a project requires it. You read exactly enough to complete the task — nothing more. Real understanding comes from building, breaking, and fixing.

### The 3-Layer Method
Each day has 3 layers:
1. **Understand** (10 min max) — just enough context
2. **Build** (core of the day) — hands-on exercise + main project task
3. **Document** (15 min) — save portfolio artifacts

### Business Scenario Rotation
You work through 5 fictional companies throughout the 45 days. Each company introduces different CRM challenges, mirroring what you'll encounter on the job.
